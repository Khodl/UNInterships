---
title: Who is eligible for UN internships?
description: Understand the academic, language, and age requirements for UN internship programs.
date: "2025-08-22"
---

Eligibility criteria vary across UN agencies, but most share a common set of requirements.

## Academic requirements

You generally need to be:

- **Currently enrolled** in a university degree program (bachelor's, master's, or PhD), **or**
- A **recent graduate** — most agencies accept candidates who graduated within the past 12 months

Some agencies (like UNICEF) accept candidates who have completed at least two years of undergraduate studies. Others (like the UN Secretariat) require enrollment in a master's or equivalent program.

## Language requirements

The United Nations has six official languages: Arabic, Chinese, English, French, Russian, and Spanish.

- **English or French fluency** is required for almost all internships
- Knowledge of additional UN languages is a strong asset
- Some duty stations (e.g., Geneva) may require French; others (e.g., Santiago) may prefer Spanish

## Age and nationality

- There is generally **no upper age limit**, though internships target students and early-career professionals
- **All nationalities** are eligible, but the UN encourages applications from underrepresented countries
- You must be able to secure a visa and cover travel costs to the duty station (some agencies assist with this)

## Other common requirements

- A valid **health insurance** policy covering the internship period
- No immediate family member currently working for the hiring UN agency
- Ability to work **full-time** during the internship period (some agencies offer part-time arrangements)
