Since 2020, many UN agencies have introduced remote and hybrid internship options. While in-person internships remain the norm, remote opportunities have become a permanent part of the system.

## Which agencies offer remote internships?

Most major agencies now list remote positions, including UNDP, UNICEF, WHO, and UN Volunteers. The UN Secretariat also offers some remote placements depending on the department.

Look for keywords like **"remote"**, **"home-based"**, or **"telework"** in the job posting.

## Advantages of remote internships

- **No relocation costs** — no visa, flights, or accommodation expenses
- **Accessible to more candidates** — especially those from developing countries who may face financial barriers
- **Flexible schedule** — some agencies allow flexible working hours across time zones

## Things to consider

- **Time zone differences** — you may need to attend meetings during your supervisor's working hours
- **Networking is harder** — in-person internships offer more exposure to UN culture and professionals
- **Equipment** — you are usually expected to provide your own laptop and internet connection
- **Not all roles are eligible** — field-based, logistics, or event-related internships typically require physical presence

## Hybrid arrangements

Some internships combine remote work with periodic visits to a UN office. These are becoming more common and offer the best of both worlds — flexibility and in-person exposure.
