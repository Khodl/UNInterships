---
title: What are UN internships?
description: An overview of internship programs across the United Nations system.
date: "2025-06-10"
---

United Nations internships are short-term opportunities offered by UN agencies, funds, and programs to students and recent graduates. They provide hands-on experience in areas such as human rights, development, peacekeeping, climate action, and public health.

## Which organizations offer internships?

Most UN entities have internship programs, including:

- **UN Secretariat** — New York, Geneva, Vienna, Nairobi
- **UNDP** — United Nations Development Programme
- **UNICEF** — United Nations Children's Fund
- **UNHCR** — UN Refugee Agency
- **WHO** — World Health Organization
- **FAO** — Food and Agriculture Organization
- **UNESCO** — UN Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization
- **ILO** — International Labour Organization
- **UNEP** — UN Environment Programme

## How long do they last?

Most UN internships last between 2 and 6 months. Some agencies allow extensions up to 12 months depending on the office and project needs.

## Are they paid?

Compensation varies by agency. The UN Secretariat began offering a monthly stipend in 2019. Other agencies like UNICEF, UNDP, and WHO also provide stipends, though amounts differ. Some smaller agencies still offer unpaid internships.
