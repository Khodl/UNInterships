title: How to apply for a UN internship?
description: Step-by-step guide to the UN internship application process.
date: "2025-07-15"
---

The application process varies by agency, but the general steps are similar across the UN system.

## 1. Check your eligibility

Most UN internships require you to be:

- Enrolled in a bachelor's, master's, or PhD program, **or** a recent graduate (within 1 year of graduation for some agencies)
- Fluent in English or French (additional UN languages are a plus: Arabic, Chinese, Russian, Spanish)
- Able to obtain a visa for the duty station if needed

## 2. Prepare your documents

You will typically need:

- An up-to-date **CV or resume** (keep it concise, 1-2 pages)
- A **cover letter** tailored to the specific position
- **Proof of enrollment** or copy of your diploma
- A **writing sample** (for some positions)
- **References** — academic or professional

## 3. Find and apply

Each agency has its own recruitment portal:

- **UN Secretariat** — [inspira.un.org](https://inspira.un.org)
- **UNDP / UNICEF / UNFPA** — [jobs.undp.org](https://jobs.undp.org)
- **WHO** — [careers.who.int](https://careers.who.int)
- **FAO** — [jobs.fao.org](https://jobs.fao.org)

Search for "internship" in the job type filter. Read the terms of reference carefully before applying.

## 4. After you apply

- Processing times range from **2 weeks to 3 months**
- You may be invited for a **video interview** or asked to complete a **written test**
- If selected, you will receive an offer letter with onboarding instructions
- Don't wait — apply to multiple positions across different agencies

## Tips for a strong application

- **Be specific** about what you can contribute, not just what you want to learn
- **Mention relevant coursework, research, or volunteer work** even if you lack professional experience
- **Show awareness of the agency's mandate** — reference specific programs or goals
- **Proofread everything** — attention to detail matters in international organizations
