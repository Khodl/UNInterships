The United Nations operates in over 190 countries. Internships are available at headquarters, regional offices, and field locations worldwide.

## Major headquarters

| City | Key agencies |
|------|-------------|
| **New York** | UN Secretariat, UNDP, UNICEF, UN Women |
| **Geneva** | UNHCR, WHO, ILO, UNCTAD, OHCHR |
| **Vienna** | UNIDO, UNODC, IAEA, CTBTO |
| **Nairobi** | UNEP, UN-Habitat |
| **Rome** | FAO, WFP, IFAD |
| **Paris** | UNESCO |
| **The Hague** | ICC, OPCW |

## Regional and country offices

Many agencies have regional hubs in cities like Bangkok, Amman, Dakar, Panama City, and Addis Ababa. Country offices in locations where the UN has development or humanitarian programs also host interns.

## Cost of living

The UN does not always cover living expenses. Consider:

- **New York and Geneva** are among the most expensive cities in the world
- **Nairobi, Bangkok, and Amman** offer more affordable living
- Some agencies provide a **stipend** that partially covers costs, but it rarely covers full expenses in expensive cities
- **Shared housing** near UN offices is common among interns — check agency-specific intern Facebook groups for leads

## Choosing a duty station

When applying, consider:

- Your **language skills** — French is essential for many Geneva and West Africa positions
- Your **area of interest** — peacekeeping roles are often in field locations; policy roles tend to be at headquarters
- **Visa requirements** — some countries are easier to access depending on your nationality
- **Career goals** — headquarters experience is valued for policy careers; field experience matters for operations roles
